## Networking Playbook

**Core strategy:**
1. Wide net of alumni — mid-level PMs and operators are often more helpful than VPs
2. Goal of the call: learn about the role and company; have them pass your resume to the hiring manager
3. Connect on something real — the introduction that sticks is specific, not generic

**3-bullet outreach format:**
- Who you are / what you're focused on at Kellogg
- Why specifically them / why now (one researched detail)
- One clear ask or question

**Questions that actually work:**
- "What would make you *not* recommend this role to a friend?"
- "What do people consistently get wrong about what this team actually does?"
- "What does a strong first 90 days look like here?"

**Always close with:** "Is there one other person you think I should talk to?"

**Play the student card.** This window closes at graduation. Use it generously now.

## Negotiation Quick Ref

Before any negotiation, prep:
- **Their profile** — what do they need? What pressures are they under?
- **My profile** — interests, priorities, non-negotiables
- **My ambition** — what does great look like, not just acceptable?
- **My differentiators** — why am I worth negotiating with?

Differentiation is leverage. The clearer you can articulate what you bring, the better your position.
