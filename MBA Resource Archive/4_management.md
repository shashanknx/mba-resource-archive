## Frameworks

### The 5 Decision Traps (MORS)
1. **Narrow Framing** — seeing complex decisions as binary. Fix: develop a "best of both worlds" option.
2. **Sampling Errors** — looking at only successes or only failures. Fix: look at the full dataset.
3. **Confirmation Bias** — seeking info that confirms what you already believe. Fix: ask disconfirming questions.
4. **Loss Aversion** — losses feel more painful than gains feel pleasurable. Fix: map the cost of *not* deciding too.
5. **Escalation of Commitment** — continuing because of sunk costs. Fix: set clear go/no-go criteria upfront; divide into stages.

Knowing about a bias doesn't remove it. You need structural fixes.

### Pre-Mortem
Before launching anything: imagine it's 12 months later and the project failed. Write the obituary. What killed it? Forces the team to surface risks that optimism and groupthink suppress.

### Influence & Power

**Sources of power:**
1. Formal/Positional · 2. Expert · 3. Referent (likability) · 4. Informational · 5. Network (broker position)

**Influence tactics:**
- **Rational persuasion** — evidence and logic; works when the other side is genuinely open
- **Coalition building** — arrive with support assembled; don't ask for it in the room
- **Reciprocity** — give before you ask; people return favors
- **Consistency** — get small yes's first; people act consistently with prior commitments
- **Framing** — same option presented as gain vs. loss lands differently
- **Legitimacy** — invoke policy or precedent to depersonalize the ask

**Leadership perception:** People evaluate you on Competence + Warmth + Professional Aura. Adapt delivery to context; don't change the substance.

**Heidi Roizen principle:** Be generously useful before you need anything. Relationships built in times of no need are the ones that pay off.

### Motivation

**Self-Determination Theory (SDT):** Three needs must all be met for sustained motivation:
- **Autonomy** — control over one's own actions
- **Competence** — feeling effective and capable
- **Relatedness** — feeling connected to others

**Expectancy Theory (Vroom):** Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence. Break any one link and motivation collapses. Managers most often miss Instrumentality — people don't see how performance leads to reward.

**Overjustification effect:** Adding extrinsic rewards to intrinsically motivating work can reduce motivation. Pay carefully.

### Story Arc (SYAI)
A story = a human navigating tension, in pursuit of something that matters.

**Five elements:** Context · Character · Conflict · Climax · Closure

Stories transfer emotion. Emotion drives action. Data alone doesn't move people.

**Story types to know:** Dread · Shame · Success · Fun · Origin · Failure · Legend · Micro · Collective Memory. Use the right type for the right moment.

**Communication principles:**
- Diagnose the room first — don't be a tone-deaf script reader
- Acknowledge emotion to build trust; evoke emotion to motivate
- Simplify your message, never your colleague's experience
- Specificity is the signal — vague compliments and vague pitches land identically: forgettably

### Negotiation

**Prep framework:**
- Employer/counterpart profile — what do they need? What pressures are they under?
- My profile — interests, priorities, non-negotiables
- My ambition — what does a great outcome look like?
- My differentiators — why am I worth negotiating with?

**Issue Matrix:**

|  | Important to me | Less important to me |
|--|----------------|----------------------|
| Easy for them to give | Trade here first | Give away freely |
| Hard for them to give | Contentious — save for last | Don't push |

Lead with easy trades. Build rapport. Save the hard issues for last.

### Consulting Structure (Analytical Consulting Lab)
1. Frame the problem — what decision are we actually helping?
2. Build the hypothesis tree — what could explain this?
3. Prioritize analyses — what gives the most signal fastest?
4. Synthesize → recommendation → implication

*Frame the problem before you solve it. Jumping to analysis before framing is the most common consulting failure.*

## Mental Models

**Reciprocity is the most durable influence tool.** Be useful before you need anything. The returns compound.

**The right story at the right time changes outcomes.** Logic gets people to nod; story gets them to act.

**"A product for everyone rarely reaches anyone."** The same principle applies to communication — a message for everyone lands with no one. Know your audience before you open your mouth.
